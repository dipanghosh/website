The world of graphic design is an wonderland to me. I have explored vector graphics to page layout, to 3D rendering. The most exciting part for me are vector pattern design and 3D rendering of biological complexes.

Below is a library of all my designs, presented in an organised way, with an insider look of how I made them! If you enjoy it, please get in touch with me!

I am hunting down my designs and adding them in here slowly, please check back sometime!

Tools I use

Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator are my primary tool of choice, Vector grapics with Illustrator and photographic designs with Photoshop. I also use Autodesk Maya for my 3D rendering designs, along with renderman or Vray as my renderer.

These tools take a while to learn an make use of. There are so may functionalities and so many things you can do with them, that the possibilities are endless. I am still continung to learn, as as the software evolves, I also have to keep up with it and change ways of working.

How to learn

Graphic design is a passion for many of us, but few find the right resources and the right guidance. These days internet is a very good resources, but I often time find that it could be difficult to find the proper resource, as there are so many of them.

I once again would recomend Lynda.com, as thats where I have learnt it all from. Initially I was learning from here and there, but a consolidated learning path makes for a much better knowldge. I suggest you take a look at the One-on-one courses by Graphics guru Deke Mclleland, he is a really good instructor, and the coursea are great.

Learning Maya and 3D design in general is a different story, and there is a very high learning curve, meaning you have a to learn a lot, before you can do anything with Maya. You have to start with Photoshop, to get your basics down, then you can start leaning Maya. There are many good courses in Lynda.com, but here, you have to practice and find your own way in.
